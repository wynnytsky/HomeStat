The HOME-STAT daily dashboard presents data from 311 service requests made by the public and the HOME-STAT canvassing team, as well as supplemental data from select organizations. The data is based on observations of people perceived to be street homeless.

The HOME-STAT monthly dashboard presents data from the City’s contracted Street Outreach Teams on HOME-STAT clients and select progress milestones and from 311. Additional reporting categories will be introduced as data becomes available.

311 Service Request Data

311 requests

311 Service Requests made by both the public and the HOME-STAT canvassing team. The number of 311 requests can vary from day to day based on multiple factors. Weather, time of day, holidays, and major events can affect the number of street homeless people who are visible as well as the number of people who may be making 311 requests. There may be overlap between requests made by HOME-STAT canvassers and the public. The accuracy of geocoded locations depends on the quality of information provided by 311 customers.

Data provided by 311, reported daily and monthly.

Public 311 Requests for Outreach Assistance

The total number of 311 service requests made by the public (calls and mobile app). Multiple requests could be made about one person and a single request could cover more than one person.

Data provided by 311, reported daily and monthly.

Requests from HOME-STAT Canvassers

The total number of 311 service requests made by HOME-STAT canvassers. HOME-STAT canvassers work between 7am and 4pm seven days a week, surveying outdoor areas including streets, sidewalks, and public spaces from Canal Street to 145th Street in Manhattan and select hot spots in the other boroughs. HOME-STAT canvassers take into account the appearance, behavior, and conditions of individuals to assess whether a person may possibly be street homeless. HOME-STAT canvassers rely on visual observations, do not engage with individuals, and do not provide outreach services.

Data provided by 311, reported daily and monthly.

Public 311 Requests routed to NYPD

The total number of 311 service requests made by the public (calls and mobile app) related to homeless persons repeatedly occupying the same location or setting up temporary dwellings. Multiple requests could be made about one person and a single request could cover more than one person. HOME-STAT canvassers do not route requests to NYPD.

Data provided by 311, reported daily and monthly.

Extreme Cold Weather

The Department of Homeless Services activates Code Blue when the National Weather Service issues a Cold Weather Alert. During Code Blue hours (4 pm to 8 am), 311 requests for homeless outreach assistance are routed directly to 911. HOME-STAT canvassers may also be re-assigned to other projects at the discretion of the Department of Homeless Services. These changes will affect the volume of 311 service requests from both the public and the HOME-STAT canvassing team.

Supplemental Data for Daily Dashboard

Supplemental data is provided by select organizations. Supplemental data is focused on areas of the City that are not covered by HOME-STAT canvassers, although these areas could be covered by Service Requests made by the public. Daily averages are used to account for varying data collection schedules.

Select Public Outdoor Spaces

An aggregated estimate of the average daily number of people perceived to be street homeless and observed in select public outdoor spaces, including select New York City parks and Business Improvement Districts.

Data provided by NYC Department of Parks and Recreation and the Alliance for Downtown New York.

Select Public Indoor Spaces

An aggregated estimate of the average daily number of people perceived to be street homeless and observed in select public indoor spaces, including select public library branches and transit hubs.

Data provided by the Mayor’s Office of Operations and the New York Public Library.

Monthly Dashboard Data Notes

Total HOME-STAT Clients

HOME-STAT clients include 1) individuals who are on the street or in transitional settings, 2) individuals who have been placed into housing and are currently receiving aftercare services, and 3) panhandlers and other individuals who are housed but appear as if they are street homeless.

Client demographics currently reported include gender, age, and veteran status. Demographic data is presented as a percentage of the HOME-STAT clients with known information.

Data provided by the Department for Homeless Services (DHS).

From Streets to Homes

The following indicators represent the location of HOME-STAT clients at a point-in-time in the reporting month:

  • “HOME-STAT clients on the street and other settings” includes individuals who were living on the street or other settings (e.g., correctional facilities or detox centers) at the end of the reporting month.
  • “HOME-STAT clients in transitional housing” includes individuals who were living in Safe Havens, stabilization beds, shelters, and other transitional housing settings at the end of the reporting month.
  • “HOME-STAT clients in permanent housing and receiving aftercare services” includes individuals who were living in supportive housing, public housing, independent living, adult home/nursing home, mental health community residence, family reunification, Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing, and other permanent housing settings at the end of the reporting month.

The following indicators represent the number of unduplicated HOME-STAT clients who were placed at any time during the reporting month in the following categories:

  • "HOME-STAT clients placed in settings other than transitional or permanent housing" includes individuals who were placed in drop-in centers, detox centers, hospitals, or intake facilities, excluding correctional facilities.
  • “HOME-STAT clients placed in transitional housing” includes individuals who were placed in safe havens, stabilization beds, shelters, and other transitional housing settings during the reporting month.
  • “HOME-STAT clients placed in permanent housing and receiving aftercare services” includes individuals who were placed in supportive housing, public housing, independent living, adult home/nursing home, mental health community residence, family reunification, Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing, and other permanent housing settings during the reporting month.

Data provided by the Department for Homeless Services (DHS).

If you see someone in need of
homeless assistance,
call 311 or use the 311 Mobile App.

Give the exact location of the individual and time you observed them. Provide details about the person’s appearance (such as age, gender, ethnicity, clothing, physical condition), behavior, and other distinguishing characteristics.

The Department of Homeless Services’ contracted Street Outreach Teams will attempt to locate the person, based on the information you provide, within an average of 1 hour. The Street Outreach Teams will offer services such as a bed in transitional housing and assistance in gathering vital documents, applying for public benefits, and receiving permanent housing.

In case of emergency, dial 911.
